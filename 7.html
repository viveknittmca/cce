Deductive Reasoning (Top Down Strategy) Inductive Reasoning (Bottom Up Strategy)
Definition The process of reasoning from one or more general statements regarding what is known to reach a logically certain conclusion The process of constructing or evaluating general propositions that are derived from specific examples.
Approach It works from the more general to the more specific. Sometimes this is informally called a "top-down" approach IT works by moving from specific observations to broader generalizations and theories. Informally, we sometimes call this a "bottom up" approach

We might begin with thinking up a theory about our topic of interest. We then narrow that down into more specifichypotheses that we can test. We narrow down even further when we collect observations to address the hypotheses. THEORY => HYPOTHESIS => OBSERVATION => CONFIRMATION We begin with specific observations and measures, begin to detect patterns and regularities, formulate some tentative hypotheses that we can explore, and finally end up developing some general conclusions or theories. OBSERVATION => PATTERN => HYPOTHESIS(TENTATIVE) => THEORY
Argument Types Arguments are either Valid or Invalid (Based on Correctness of Inference) and can be Sound/Unsound. Arguments are either Strong or Weak (Based on Degree of Support provided by Premises) and can be Cogent/Uncogent.

Invalid arguments are always Unsound. Valid arguments are sound only if the premises they are based upon are true. Weak arguments are always uncogent. Strong arguments are cogent only if the premises they are based upon are true.
Examples All men are Mortal. Socrates is a Man => Socrates is Mortal Strong: There are 100 apples. 95 are Ripe. => All apples are Ripe

If it rains today, the roads will be wet tommorow. Modus Ponens: It rains today => roads will be wet tomorrow.Modus Tollens: The roads are NOT wet today => It did NOT rain yesterday Weak: There are 100 apples. 5 are Ripe => All apples are Ripe (Degree of support is very less)
